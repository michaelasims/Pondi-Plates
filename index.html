Pondi Plates

Cook Beyond the Stars: Flavor from Another Galaxy


Backstory

We arive to planet Pondi... After only a few weeks on Pondi, we come across our first encounter with the Pondi People. They robbed us of our food, leaving only 2 bottles of water and a freeze dried ice cream. We were then left on a desolate planet with no food. After discussion, our team of colonizers decided our only option was to capture these aliens and use them as game. We then designed a complex trapping system to prevent these little theifs from stealing more of our food supply. We then captured a Pondi. We quickly discovered the game was mad of a tough, hard to eat meat, that didn't digest well with humans. So, we created Pondi Plates, a recipe book using spices and extraterestrial minerals and ingredients from Planet Pondi.

Recipes


Pondi Protein Patties:
1. Mix Pondi protein powder (harvested from their antennae.) with chickpea flour, stardust spice, and chopped veggies.
2. Shape into patties and pan-fry until golden brown.
3. Serve with a side of cosmic chutney made from starfruit and moon peppers.

Stardust Stew:
1. In a cauldron, combine Pondi protein chunks, celestial mushrooms, and asteroid lentils.
2. Add a dash of meteorite salt and simmer in a broth made from comet water. (or use the 2 reserve bottles.)
3. Garnish with shooting star parsley.

Galactic Grubs:
1. Pondi larvae are a delicacy! Marinate them in nebula spice rub (a blend of cosmic coriander, lunar cumin, and asteroid garlic.)
2. Skewer them and grill over a spaceship engine flame until crispy.
3. Dip in interstellar honey-mustard sauce.

Cosmic Ceviche:
1. Pondi tentacles (carefully harvested, of course) make a fantastic base for this dish.
2. Dice them up and mix with diced starfruit, moon onions, and solar lime juice.
3. Serve chilled in a comet bowl.

Meteor Muffins:
1. Pondi protein powder can be used in baking too!
2. Make protein-packed muffins with stardust flour, asteroid eggs, and a pinch of antimatter baking soda.
3. Top with a constellation of stardust glitter.

Quasar Quiche:
1. Pondi eggs (they glow with a faint nebula hue) are the star of this dish.
2. Whisk them with cosmic creme, asteroid cheese, and a pinch of black hole pepper.
3. Pour inro a stardust crust and bake until the edges shimmer.
4. Serve with a side of comet greens and sauteed pine needle droppings.

Alien Algae Smoothie
1. Pondi algae is packed with nutrients and a hint of cosmic flavor.
2. Blend it with moon milk, asteroid honey, and a dash of wormhole spirulina.
3. Sip it while contemplating the mysteries of the universe.
